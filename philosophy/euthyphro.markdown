# Outline of Argument

1. What is holy and unholy? Is there a single characteristic that all pious (things) share and all impious (things) lack?
    * In addition, Euthyphro agrees that whatever is pious cannot be impious, and whatever is impious cannot be pious.

2. What's pious is this: Prosecuting those who commit an injustice, or those who've done some other such wrong. Not prosecuting them is impious.
  * But that's just listing things that are pious. Is there a form itself by virtue of which all the pieties (pious actions) are pious?

3. What's loved by the gods is pious, and what's not loved by the gods is impious.
  * Gods quarrel with each other.
       * These issues that they quarrel about are not easily settled with objective measurement. 
       * The issues that humans argue about are the just and the unjust, fine and shameful, good and bad. These issues on which disagreemnt and inability to find a settlement make enemies of humans.
       * And according to Greek mythology, different sets of gods consider different things to be just or unjust, fine or shameful, good or bad.
       * Therefore, the same thing can be both hated and loved by the gods, so one thing would be pious and impious, which is a contradiction.
  * Euthyphro responds by stating that in the case of murder, all gods agree that it's impious.
       * Men don't argue about whether someone who killed unjustly should be punished. Rather, they argue whether someone killed justly or unjustly. Men don't dare to say that an someone who killed unjustly should not be punished.
       * Similarly, gods also argue about actions, whether they are just or unjust, fine or shameful, good or bad.
       * So how can you prove in court that all gods would agree with you that your action is just?

4. What *all* gods are pious, what *all* gods hate are impious, and what some gods like and others hate is neither.
  * Is the pious loved by the gods because it's pious, or is it pious because it's loved?
       * A carried thing is a carried thing because it's carried. In other words, a carried thing is carried not because it's carriable, but because it's carried by someone. 
       * Similarly, a loved thing is not loved because it's love-able, but because someone loves it.
       * Euthyphro, however, agrees that a thing is not pious because it's loved, rather a thing is god-loved because it's pious.
       * Therefore, "pious" and "god-loved" is not the same thing, since something is god-loved because it's loved by the gods, but something is not pious simply because it's loved by the gods. One of them is lovable because it's loved, whereas the other is loved because it's loveable.

5. Is the pious a part of the just?
  * Euthyphro establishes that whatever is pious is just.
  * Not everytying that's just is pious. 
       * Where there's shame, there is dread. But it's not true that where there is dread, there is shame. For example, disease and poverty are dreadful but not shameful.
       * So shame is broader than dread, so shame is a part of fear.
  * Therefore, pious is a part of just. 

6. That part of the just that is concerned with tending to gods is pious, while the remaining part is concerned with tending to humans.
  * When something is being tended to, the same end is being accomplished: the good or benefit of what's being tended to.
  * Then tending to the gods would mean that being pious means benefiting or making a god better. Euthyphro does not agree.

7. The pious is a part of the just that is a service to the gods.
  * A service aims to produce something.
       * A doctor's service is to produce health. A shipbuilder's service is to produce a ship. A general's service is produce victories.
  * Then what is the main result that the services to the gods produce?

8. Piety would be knowing how to ask from the gods and how to give to them.
  * Asking for things from the gods would mean to ask things that we need from them. 
  * So giving things to gods would mean to give things that they need from us.
  * Therefore gods benefit from what they receive from us.
       * If these gifts are honor and reverence, which is what they like, then we're back at saying that piousness is doing things the gods love.
